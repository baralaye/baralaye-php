Biography

Ebitenyefa Baralaye is a sculptor and designer. He received an MFA in Ceramics from the Cranbrook Academy of Art (2016) and a BFA in Ceramics from the Rhode Island School of Design (2006). With a history spanning Nigeria, the Caribbean and the United States, his work abstracts conditions of desire and identity around themes of spirituality, ethnicity, utility and personal narrative. Ebitenyefa has held residencies at the Peters Valley Crafts Center in Layton, NJ, The Elizabeth Foundation for the Arts in NYC and Talking Dolls in Detroit, MI. His work has been in various domestic and international exhibitions including the 2011 Gyeonggi International Ceramix Biennale and the 2015 Architectural Digest Home Design Show. He is currently represented by David Klein Gallery in Detroit.
